# Scalar Products

$$a \cdot b=|a||b|\cos \theta$$
Properties: 
- $a\cdot b=b\cdot a$
- If $a$ lies in a direction perpendicular to that of $b$ then $a\cdot b=0$ and the two vectors are said to be orthogonal to each other (i.e. $a$ is orthogonal to $b$ and vice-versa $b$ is orthogonal to $a$)
- If $a\cdot b=0$ then either
	- $a$ and $b$ are orthogonal
	- one (or both) of $a$ and $b$ is a zero vector 
- $a\cdot a=|a|^2$
- $a\cdot(b+c)=a\cdot b+a\cdot c$
- $(ma)\cdot b=m(a\cdot b)$ for all $m \in \mathbb{R}$

![[Pasted image 20260112024626.png]]![[Pasted image 20260112024630.png]]

In conclusion other way to express the scalar product: 
$$\begin{align*}
a&=a_{1}i+a_{2}j+a_{3}k\\
b&=b_{1}i+b_{2}j+b_{3}k\\
a\cdot b&=a_{1}b_{1}+a_{2}b_{2}+a_{3}b_{3}
\end{align*}$$
Scalar products can be used to find angles:
$$\cos \theta=\frac{a\cdot b}{|a||b|}$$

# Cross Product

![[Pasted image 20260112025412.png]]
![[Pasted image 20260112025434.png]]
$$a \wedge b=|a||b|\sin \theta$$
Properties:
- $a \wedge b=-b\wedge a$
- If $a\wedge b=0$ then either
	- $a$ and $b$ are parallel 
	- one (or both) of $a$ and $b$ is a zero vector
- $a\wedge(b+c)=a\wedge b+b\wedge c$
- $(k_{1}a)\wedge(k_{2}b)=(k_{1}k_{2})(a\wedge b)$ for all $k_{1},\ k_{2} \in \mathbb{R}$
