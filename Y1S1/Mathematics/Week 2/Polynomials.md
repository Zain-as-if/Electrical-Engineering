<h4>Remainder Theorem</h4>
In each case we note that the remainder after dividing a polynomial $p(x)$ by $x-a$ is $p(a)$,

i.e. the value of the polynomial evaluated at $x=a$
Thus in example: $$\begin{align*}p(x)&=2x^2+3x+5 \\ p(-5)&=2(-5)^2+3(-5)+5 \\ &=50-15+5 \\ &=40\end{align*}$$
If $p(x)$ is any polynomial and $a$ is any number then:$$p(x)=q(x)(x-a)+p(a)$$
where $q(x)$ is a polynomial of order one less than $p(x)$. The remainder is $p(a)$

If '$a$' is a root of a polynomial, then $p(a)=0$

<h4>Factor Theorem</h4>
If $a$ is a root of a polynomial $p(x), (x-a)$ is a factor,
i.e. $p(x)=q(x)(x-a)$
where $q(x)$ is a polynomial of order one less than $p(x)$
