# Mechanism That Can Produce An EMF 

## Motional Induction

### Moving B-field, Static Wire

A **stationary** conductor in a moving magnetic field
- referred to as **motional** induction
- static wire, moving B-field
![[Pasted image 20260111013209.png]]
Whilst magnet and loop are stationary no current is observed
![[Pasted image 20260111013228.png]]
Direction of induced E-field (and therefore current) is such as to oppose the change producing it (Lenz's law)
In this case current produces a magnetic field with N-pole to the right of the loop and S-pole to the left
- Use RH screw rule (Or RH grasp rule) to get B-field direction from $i$

### Static B-field, Moving Wire

A **moving** conductor in a (stationary) unchanging magnetic field
- also **motional** induction
- static B-field, moving wire
![[Pasted image 20260111013357.png]]
Whilst magnet and loop are stationary no current is observed 
![[Pasted image 20260111013415.png]]
Wire loop moving toward magnet

Direction of induced E-field (and therefore current) is such as to oppose the change producing it (Lenz's law)
In this case current produces a magnetic field with N-pole to the right and S-pole to the left

## Transformer Induction

### Increasing Magnetic Field

A stationary conductor in an **increasing** magnetic field produced by a stationary field source
- referred to as **transformer** induction
- static wire, changing B-field
![[Pasted image 20260111013626.png]]
Direction of induced E-field (and therefore current) is such as to oppose the change producing it (Lenz's law) 
In this case current produces a magnetic field with N-pole to the right of loop to **oppose** the **increasing** flux coming from right 
- Use RH screw rule (Or RH grasp rule) to get B-field direction from $i$

### Decreasing Magnetic Field

A stationary conductor in a **decreasing** magnetic field produced by a stationary field source
- referred to as **transformer** induction
- static wire, changing B-field 
![[Pasted image 20260111013959.png]]
Direction of induced E-field (and therefore current) is such as to oppose the change producing it (Lenz's law)
In this case current produces a magnetic field with S-pole to the right of loop to **reinforce** the **decreasing** flux coming from the right 
- Use RH screw rule (Or RH grasp rule) to get B-field direction from $i$

# Direction Of EMF

A moving (changing) conductor in an unchanging (stationary) magnetic field
- referred to as **motional** induction
- static B-field, moving wire
![[Pasted image 20260111014204.png]]
Direction of induced E-field (and therefore current) is such as to try to keep the flux through the loop **constant**
In this case current produces a magnetic field through centre of loop pointing to the right (**opposing** the **increasing** flux pointing to left) and therefore trying to keep flux constant 

# Faraday's Law & Lenz's Law

Induced EMF around a closed loop $N=1$ is equal to a rate of decrease (**minus** rate of **increase**) of magnetic flux linking the loop 
![[Pasted image 20260111014359.png]]
To establish direction of $v_{emf}$ **using this equation** we have to relate a direction around the loop to a direction of the flux **change** $\frac{d\varphi}{dt}$ (which is at right angles to the loop)

Start by establishing the direction of the flux **change** (i.e. direction of $\frac{d\varphi}{dt}$)
If flux linking the loop is **increasing** then direction of $\frac{d\varphi}{dt}$ is in the same direction as $\varphi$
If flux linking the loop is **decreasing** then direction of $\frac{d\varphi}{dt}$ is in the opposite direction as $\varphi$
Direction of $v_{emf}$ is opposite to (minus) the rotational direction given by a right-hand screw pointing in the direction of flux **change**

## Faraday's Law For A Coil

For N-turn coil, emf is induced each turn

Total emf between the ends of coil is therefore N times that for a single turn (or loop) i.e.:
$$v_{emf}=-N \frac{d\varphi}{dt}\ [V]$$
Two explanations (same explanation expressed two different ways):
- Either flux cuts the surface bounded by the wire of the coil N times
- Or the flux is used N times, once by each loop and the emfs from each loop

# Flux Linkage

Flux linkage ($\Lambda$) is not just the flux going through a coil
It is the sum over all loops of the flux flowing through each loop
$$\Lambda=\varphi_{1}+\varphi_{2}+\dots+\varphi_{N}=\sum_{n=1}^N\varphi_{i}\ (Wb)$$
![[Pasted image 20260111015558.png]]
If (but only if) all the flux goes through all the turns then: $$\Lambda=N\varphi(Wb)$$
Flux not linking the coils is called flux leakage

# Mutual Inductance

Mutual inductance ($M_{21}$) = $\frac{magnetic\ flux\ linking\ circuit\ 2}{current\ flowing\ in\ circuit\ 1}$
![[Pasted image 20260111015751.png]]
$$M_{21}=\frac{\Lambda_{2}}{I_{1}}\ (Wb/A\ or\ henries,\ H)$$
Flux linkage is not just the flux; it is the sum over all loops of the flux flowing through each loop

Mutual inductance is when all flux flows through all turns (i.e. in absence of flux leakage)
If flux is flowing through all turns of circuit 2 is equal to flux, $\varphi$, produced by circuit 1 then:
$$\Lambda_{2}=N_{2}\varphi\ (Wb)$$
And:
$$M_{21}=\frac{\Lambda_{2}}{I_{1}}=\frac{N_{2}\varphi}{I_{1}}\ (Wb/A\ or\ H)$$
![[Pasted image 20260111020018.png]]

# Voltage Developed Across Mutual Inductance

If fluxes flowing through all turns of circuit 2 are equal (to each other and to the flux, $\varphi$, produced by circuit 1) then from Faraday's law:
$$v_{emf_{2}}=-N_{2} \frac{d\varphi}{dt}=-N_{2}A \frac{dB}{dt}\ (V)$$
But:
$$M_{21}=\frac{\Lambda_{2}}{i_{1}}=\frac{N_{2}BA}{i_{1}}\ (Wb/A\ or\ H)$$
Therefore:
$$B=\frac{M_{21}}{N_{2}A}i_{1}\ (Wb/m^2)$$
So:
$$\frac{dB}{dt}=\frac{M_{21}}{N_{2}A} \frac{di_{1}}{dt}$$ Or:
$$\frac{d\varphi}{dt}=\frac{M_{21}}{N_{2}} \frac{di_{1}}{dt}$$
![[Pasted image 20260111020257.png]]
So:
$$v_{emf_{2}}=-M_{21} \frac{di_{1}}{dt}$$

## Back EMF

![[Pasted image 20260111021154.png]]
Induced voltage is referred to as a back emf
- possibly because if current is **increasing** (due to increasing driving emf) induced voltage is in opposite (back) direction to driving emf
More properly we should consider it a back emf against the direction of $\frac{di}{dt}$ 
$$v_{emf}=-L \frac{di}{dt}$$
## Physical Explanation Of Back EMF

![[Pasted image 20260111021419.png]]
- Current changes in loop 1
- Magnetic flux ($\varphi$) flowing through loop 1 changes
- Changing $\varphi$ created by loop 1 flows through loop 2
- Changing $\varphi$ in loop 2 (created by loop 1) causes changing electric flux ($\psi$) to flow in closed loops around changing magnetic flux 
- Direction of electric field (E), and thus direction of current in loop 2, is such as to keep $\varphi$ through loop 2 constant
- Integral of E around each loop results in emf (voltage) around each loop which sum to $v_{emf}$ across coil
- Direction of $v_{emf}$ such as to keep current constant 

# Behaviours Of Inductors

## Low Frequency Behaviours Of Inductors

$$v_{emf}=-L \frac{di}{dt}$$
Equation tells us that if current is unchanging (i.e. is 0 Hz or DC) no voltage exists across inductor 
- an inductor is therefore a short-circuit to DC
- and is 'nearly' a short-circuit (i.e. has low reactance) at low frequency 
![[Pasted image 20260111021532.png]]

## High Frequency Behaviours Of Inductors

$$v_{emf}=-L \frac{di}{dt}$$
Equation tells us that if current is changing rapidly (i.e. has a high frequency) a large back emf will resist the (changing) current
- an inductor is approx. an open circuit (i.e. has high impedance) at high frequency
- if current changes rapidly (e.g. by being switched off) emf can rise to dangerous values
![[Pasted image 20260111021708.png]]

# Hydraulic Analogy For Inductor

Heavy water wheel or turbine resistant to change in water flow (i.e. resistant to AC)

Stores energy in angular momentum of wheel

Hard to push water through turbine until it has been accelerated 

When pump pressure (EMF) increases inertia of water wheel resists increase in current

Passes DC easily once wheel has been accelerated to required rate

When pump pressure (EMF) decreases (or is turned off) turbine acts as pump due to stored angular momentum and water flow (current) persists

Stored kinetic energy of rotating turbine analogous to stored magnetic field energy

Represents DC 'short circuit' but an AC block in high frequency limit

# Flyback Diodes

![[Pasted image 20260111022019.png]]
As switch opens attempt it being made to change current rapidly to zero

Back emf rises to high levels to attempt to maintain current flow

Fly back diode is reverse biased and therefore irrelevant to circuit operation when switch is closed

When switch is opened, induced emf (in direction to keep current flowing) drives current around diode circuit and falls gradually to zero without damaging circuit

# Stored Energy In Inductor 

![[Pasted image 20260111022706.png]]

Consider current $i(t)$ rising linearly from 0 at $t=0$ to $I$ at $t=T$ and then remaining constant with time for $t>T$
- All energy ($W_{m}$) goes into magnetic field because where else could energy go
$$\begin{align*}
W_{m}&=\int_{t=0}^Tp(t)\ dt=\int_{0}^Ti(t)v(t)\ dt\\
&=\int_{0}^Ti(t)L \frac{d}{dt}[i(t)]\ dt=L\int_{0}^Ti \frac{di}{dt}\ dt\\
&\text{when}\ t=T,\ i(t)=I\\
&=L\int_{0}^Ii\ di=L\left[ \frac{i^2}{2} \right]_{0}^I=\frac{1}{2}LI^2
\end{align*}$$
No further energy added to field for $t>T$ since $v=L \frac{di}{dt}=0$

External circuit measures inductance and produces an electrical signal whose amplitude is related to rod displacement
