# Normalised Power

Ignores resistance: $P=I^2(A^2)=V^2$
Or assumes resistance is 1 ohm:
$$P=I^2=V^2$$

## Instantaneous Normalised Power 

![[Pasted image 20260110001318.png]]
$$\Large p(t)=v^2(t)=v_{p}^2\sin^2(\omega t)=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}[1-\cos(2\omega t)]$$
## Digression 

Trigonometric Identity
$$\sin^2(x)\equiv \frac{1}{2}[1-\cos(2x)]$$
Other trigonometric identity that may be useful
$$\cos^2(x)\equiv \frac{1}{2}[1+\cos(2x)]$$
![[Pasted image 20260110001542.png]]

What is the mean (time-averaged) normalised power?

By inspection:
$$\langle p(t)\rangle=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}$$
By calculation:
$$\begin{align*} \langle p(t)\rangle&=\langle v^2(t)\rangle=\langle v_{p}^2\sin^2(\omega t)\rangle=v_{p}^2 \left\langle \frac{1}{2}[1-\cos(2\omega t)] \right\rangle\\
&=v_{p}^2 \frac{1}{2}\langle[1-\cos(2\omega t)]\rangle=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}[1-\langle\cos(2\omega t)\rangle]=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}[1-0]=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}
\end{align*}$$
# Normalised Energy

W is used to denote energy because $E$ is used to denote electric field strength
[[11. AC Signal Period, Frequency, Phase & Mean Value#Mean Of Waveforms]]
Multiply by 2 in 2nd line as limit changed from $T$ -> $T/2$ 


$$\Large \begin{align*}
W=\int_{0}^T p(t)\ dt&=\int_{0}^T v^2(t)\ dt\\
&=2\int_{0}^{\frac{T}{2}}v^2(t)\ dt\\
&=2\int_{0}^{\frac{T}{2}}v_{p}^2\sin^2(\omega t)\ dt\\
&=2v_{p}^2\int_{0}^{\frac{T}{2}} \frac{1}{2}[1-\cos(2\omega t)] \ dt\\
&=v_{p}^2\left\{ \int_{0}^{\frac{T}{2}}1\ dt-\int_{0}^{\frac{T}{2}}\cos(2\omega t)\ dt \right\}\\&=v_{p}^2\left\{ [t]_{0}^{\frac{T}{2}}-0  \right\}\\
&=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}T
\end{align*}$$![[Pasted image 20260110003041.png]]
# Normalised Mean Power 

Calculated as energy dissipated per second (energy/time)

$$\begin{align*}
W&=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}T\\
P&=\langle p(t)\rangle=\frac{W}{T}=\frac{\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}T}{T}=\frac{v_{p}^2}{2}
\end{align*}$$

What DC quantity dissipates same power as a sinusoidal AC quantity? 
$$\Large \begin{align*}
V_{effective}&=\sqrt{ P }\\
&=\sqrt{ \langle p(t)\rangle }\\
&=\sqrt{ \langle v^2(t)\rangle }\\
&=V_{\text{rms}}
\end{align*}$$
$V_{\text{rms}}$ is the (square) root of the mean of the square referred to as the 'root mean square'
It is the DC voltage (or current) that would dissipate the same power as the AC waveform 

$\langle\rangle$ denotes **time average**

# Calculation Of RMS Value

Correct for any waveform (including sinusoids)
$$V_{\text{rms}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^Tv^2(t)\ dt}$$

Correct only for sinusoids
$$V_{\text{rms}}=\frac{v_{p}}{\sqrt{ 2 }}\cong 0.7071v_{p}$$

0.5$v_{p}^2$ is mean average square 

$$v(t)=v_{p}\sin(\omega t)$$
$$V_{\text{rms}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^Tv^2(t)\ dt}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^T[v_{p}\sin(\omega t)]^2\ dt}$$
![[Pasted image 20260110004323.png]]
$$V_{\text{rms}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^T[v_{p}\sin(\omega t)]^2\ dt}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^Tv_{p}^2 \frac{1}{2}[1-\cos(2\omega t)]\ dt}$$

$$V_{\text{rms}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^Tv_{p}^2 \frac{1}{2}[1-\cos(2\omega t)]\ dt}=\sqrt{ \frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^Tv_{p}^2\left[  \frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{2}\cos(2\omega t) \right]\ dt }$$
![[Pasted image 20260110004602.png]]
$$\begin{align*}V_{\text{rms}}&=\sqrt{ \frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^T \frac{v_{p}^2}{2}\ dt-\int_{0}^T \frac{v_{p}^2}{2}\cos(2\omega t)\ dt}=\sqrt{ \frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^T \frac{v_{p}^2}{2}\ dt}\\
V_{\text{rms}}&=\sqrt{ \frac{1}{T}\left[ \frac{v_{p}^2}{2}t \right]_{0}^T}=\sqrt{ \frac{1}{T}\left[ \frac{v_{p}^2}{2}T-0 \right]}=\sqrt{ \left[ \frac{v_{p}^2}{2} \right]}=\frac{v_{p}}{\sqrt{2}}
\end{align*}$$

# Nominal UK Domestic Power Supply

![[Pasted image 20260110005204.png]]
Frequency: 
$$f=\frac{1}{T}=50\ Hz\ (\omega=2\pi f=314.2\ rads^{-1})$$
$$V_{\text{rms}}=\frac{v_{pk}}{\sqrt{ 2 }}=240\ V\ \text{(nominal)}$$
Nominal peak voltage of domestic mains power is thus $$240\times \sqrt{2}=339\ V$$
$57.6\ \Omega$ is approx 1 bar of a typical electric (radiant) fire
Nominal power dissipated by $57.6\ \Omega$ resistive load is: 
$$P=\frac{V_{\text{rms}}^2}{R}=\frac{240^2}{57.6}=1000\ W$$
RMS current drawn by $57.6\ \Omega$ resistive load is: $$I_{\text{rms}}=\frac{V_{\text{rms}}}{R}=\frac{240}{57.6}=4.17\ A$$
# Current Statutory Limits Of UK Domestic Power Supply

![[Pasted image 20260110005549.png]]
Frequency: 
$$f=\frac{1}{T}=50\ Hz\ (\text{with statutory limits of}\ \pm 1\%)$$
Statutory maximum and minimum frequencies thus $50.5\ Hz$ and $49.5\ Hz$

Voltage:
$$V_{\text{rms}}=\frac{v_{pk}}{\sqrt{ 2 }}=230\ V\ (\text{with statutory limits}\ +10\%, -6\%)$$
Note that the historical $240\ V$ is $4.3\%$ above nominal voltage and thus is within current statutory limits

Statutory maximum and minimum peak amplitudes of domestic mains power are thus: 
$$\begin{align*}
230\times 1.1\times \sqrt{ 2 }&=357.8\ V\\
230\times 0.94\times \sqrt{ 2 }&=305.8\ V
\end{align*}$$
