# Amplitude, Period, Frequency & Phase

(Cyclical) Frequency, $f=\frac{1}{T}$ Cycles per second (cps) or Hertz (Hz)
(Radian) Frequency, $\omega=\frac{2\pi}{T}$, Radians per second (rad/s)
Phase angle, $\varphi=\frac{\tau}{T}2\pi$ radians (rad)
Phase angle, $\varphi=\frac{\tau}{T}360$ degrees ($\circ$)
![[Pasted image 20260109234706.png]]
Cyclical frequency $f=\frac{1}{T}$ referred to as 'fundamental' frequency of any periodic waveform
- If waveform is not sinusoid it contains other, higher, frequencies in addition to the fundamental frequency

# Parameters Defining A Sinusoid

$$v(t)=V_{p}\cos(\omega t+\varphi)$$
Where:
$v(t)$ = Instantaneous voltage at time t 
$V_{p}$ = Peak (i.e. maximum) amplitude (voltage in this case)
$\omega$ = Angular Frequency (rad/s)
$\omega t$ = Instantaneous angle at time t
$\varphi$ = A fixed angle

# Hydrological Analogy

Flow of electrons in conductor similar to water molecules flowing in a pipe

In AC, water flow alternates in direction
- Electrons just oscillate about some mean position
- For a sinusoidal wave they oscillate sinusoidally 
- Instantaneous direction of (conventional) current opposite to instantaneous direction of electrons

# Mean Of Waveforms

![[Pasted image 20260109235140.png]]
Integral gives the (net) area under one period of waveform

Dividing by $T$ gives the height of the rectangle of equal area 

For the  triangular waveform above $v_{mean}=0$ by inspection 

Some tricks to make this easier: 
![[Pasted image 20260109235249.png]]

If a waveform has appropriate symmetry use this and calculate area (i.e. integrate) over $\frac{1}{2}$ or $\frac{1}{4}$period (or any other fraction) and multiply answer by 2 or 4 (i.e. the reciprocal of the fraction)
- For waveform above integrate from $t=-1\ ms$ to $2\ ms$ and multiply by 2

$$\Large \begin{align*}

v_{mean}=\langle v(t)\rangle&=2\times \frac{1}{6\times 10^{-3}}\int_{-1\times 10^{-3}}^{2\times 10^{-3}}\frac{3}{2\times 10^{-3}}t\ dt\\
