# Conductance

By taking the reciprocal of the resistance of a material we have a measure of how well the material conducts electricity 

Conductance is denoted G with units of siemens (S)$$G=\frac{1}{R}\ (S)$$
If resistance is High, conductivity is low
If resistance is Low, conductivity is high

Conductance is useful for analysing parallel circuits

Total conductance:$$G_{T}=G_{1}+G_{2}+\dots+G_{N}$$
# Kirchhoff's Current Law

[[5. Series Circuits, KVL & Internal Resistance#Kirchhoff's Voltage Law]]

Sum of currents leaving or entering an area, junction or system is zero$$\sum I=0$$
The sum of currents entering an area, system or junction must equal the sum of currents leaving $$\sum I_{\text{entering}}=\sum I_{\text{leaving}}$$

# Current Divider Rule

For two parallel elements of equal value, the current will divide equally 

For parallel elements with different values, the smaller the resistance, the greater the share of curent

For parallel elements of different values, the current will split/divide with a ratio equal to the inverse of the resistor values$$I_{x}=I_{T} \frac{R_{T}}{R_{x}}$$
E.g. 1
![[Pasted image 20260108044210.png]]
$$\begin{align*}
\frac{1}{R_{T}}&=\frac{1}{6}+\frac{1}{3}=\frac{3}{6}\\
R_{T}&=\frac{6}{3}=2\ \Omega\\
I_{x}&=I_{T} \frac{R_{T}}{R_{x}}\\
I_{1}&=6\times \left( \frac{2}{6} \right) = 2\ A\\
I_{2}&=6\times \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) = 4\ A
\end{align*}$$

# Voltage Sources

<h4>Voltage Sources In Series</h4>

Why connect voltage sources in series: 
- Voltages add - good if component has certain working voltage
- Maximum current is still the same (e.g. 10 amp-hours)
![[Pasted image 20260108045000.png]]

<h4>Voltage Sources In Parallel</h4>

Why connect voltage sources in parallel:
- Double the current capacity (good if component needs high current)
- Voltage across parallel elements is the same
- Can be placed in parallel only if they have the same voltage
![[Pasted image 20260108045057.png]]

Shouldn't connect batteries of different voltages in parallel
Higher voltage battery will discharge into the weaker battery
![[Pasted image 20260108045210.png]]

The higher voltage battery will discharge into the lower voltage battery until they both have the same voltage 

In this case voltages will equilibrate at 9 V

Current flowing between the batteries will be very high ![[Pasted image 20260108045329.png]]
