# Resistance, R

Resistors convert electrical energy to heat $$\text{Opposition}=\frac{\text{Cause}}{\text{Effect}}$$
The cause is our voltage - the potential energy (or pressure)
Effect we're trying to get is the current (I)
Resistance is the opposition encountered 

Resistance converts electrical energy to heat 
Value of resistance depends on: 
- Material - Depends on the atomic structure
- Length - The longer the path the greater the resistance
- Cross-Sectional Area - Thinner it is the higher chance of electrons hitting each other
- Temperature - Atoms vibrate more at higher temperature

At room temperature (optimal temperature) 
Low level of atomic vibration = Low resistance

At Higher temperatures 
High level of atomic vibration = High resistance

# Resistivity

Intrinsic property of that material (not shape dependent)
- Larger the resistivity the harder it is to get current to flow 
$$\rho=\frac{RA}{L}$$
$\rho$ is resistivity ($\Omega\ m$) 
L is length ($m$)
A is cross-sectional area ($m^2$)

# Ohms Law

Defines the relationship between Voltage, Resistance, Current
A component obeys ohms law when the current increases linearly with voltage$$V=IR$$
Polarity: Current flows from +ve to -ve ends of components
Electron Flow: Electrons flow from -ve to +ve ends of components

# Measuring Current

# Measuring Voltage

Voltmeter - measures voltage (connected in parallel, high resistance)

# Measuring Resistance

Ohmmeter - measures resistance

Performed when circuit is **off** - makes its own circuit with the component being measured
Ohmmeter applies a voltage across the component, measures the current and that gives the resistance of the component

# Power

Power = Rate of doing work
Indicates how much energy is dissipated per second $$P=\frac{W}{t}\ \text{Joules/second or Watts\ (W)}$$
$$1\ \text{hp}=746\ W$$
![[Pasted image 20260107042341.png]]
![[Pasted image 20260107042445.png]]

If current through, or voltage across resistor is greater than its ability to dissipate heat then it will melt (i.e. 'burn out')

Resistors therefore rated for maximum power

# Temperature Coefficient of resistance (TCR)

Specifies how much resistor value changes as its temperature changes from a reference value

Usually expressed in $ppm/^\circ C$
- Parts per million per degree Centigrade

Reference temperature usually $20 ^\circ C$

Usually TCR is positive (resistance gets greater as temperature increases) but can be negative

Specified by 6th band 

Example
![[Pasted image 20260107043017.png]]
What is power being delivered to motor in HP
$$\begin{align*}
V&=IR\\
R&=\frac{120}{5}\\
R&=24\ \Omega\\
P&=I^2R\\
P&=5^2 \times 24\\
P&=600\ W\\
\therefore \frac{600}{746} &= 0.8\ hp
\end{align*}$$
# Efficiency

No system is 100% efficient 
$$\begin{align*}
W_{in}&=W_{out}+W_{lost}+W_{stored}\\
P_{in}&=P_{out}+P_{lost}+P_{stored}
\end{align*}$$

![[Pasted image 20260107043949.png]]$$\text{Efficiency}\ (\eta)=\frac{\text{Power Output}\ (P_{o})}{\text{Power Input}\ (P_{i})}\ (\times 100\ \text{for \%})$$
![[Pasted image 20260107044212.png]]

Efficiency of the entire system is the product of all the individual efficiencies: $$\eta_{T}=\eta_{1}\eta_{2}\eta_{3}$$
Where $$\eta_{1}=\frac{P_{o1}}{P_{i 1}},\ \eta_{2}=\frac{P_{o 2}}{P_{i 2}},\ \eta_{3}=\frac{P_{o 3}}{P_{i 3}}$$
