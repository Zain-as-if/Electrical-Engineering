Useful for finding unknown voltages in circuits with current sources

Uses KCL to find the unknown voltages

## Techniques

1. Determine number of nodes in circuit and choose a reference node (usually ground)
2. Label all other nodes apart from reference node ($V_{1}, V_{2}, \dots$)
3. Assume all unknown current directions are away from the node under consideration
4. Apply KCL to each node
5. Solve resulting sets of simultaneous equations

## Examples

### E.g. 1

![[Pasted image 20260109222005.png]]

Step 1:
Pick reference node (ground)
- Node is a junction of two or more branches
![[Pasted image 20260109222030.png]]
Network has 2 nodes
Lower node will be defined as the reference 
- Ground Voltage (0 V)
Other node will be defined as $V_{1}$
- Voltage from $V_{1}$ to ground

Step 2 & 3:
Label all other nodes $V_{1}$ (apart from reference)
Assume all unknown current directions are away from the node under consideration ($V_{1}$) 
![[Pasted image 20260109222311.png]]

Step 4:
Apply KCL to each node
![[Pasted image 20260109222357.png]]
$V_{1}$ in equations below is the voltage across both $R_{1}$ and $R_{2}$ as they are parallel 
$V_{R_{1}}=V_{1}-E$ as the voltage across $R_{1}$ is the voltage difference between $V_{1}$ and $E$
$$\begin{align*}
I&=I_{R_{1}}+I_{R_{2}}\\
I_{R_{2}}&=\frac{V_{R_{2}}}{R_{2}}=\frac{V_{1}}{R_{2}}\\
I_{R_{1}}&=\frac{V_{R_{1}}}{R_{1}}=\frac{V_{1}-E}{R_{1}}\\
I&=\frac{V_{1}-E}{R_{1}}+\frac{V_{1}}{R_{2}}
\end{align*}$$
Step 5:
Solve the resulting simultaneous equations 
(in this example only one equation)
$$\begin{align*}
I&=\frac{V_{1}}{R_{1}}-\frac{E}{R_{1}}+\frac{V_{1}}{R_{2}}\\
1&=\frac{V_{1}}{6}-\frac{24}{6}+\frac{V_{1}}{12}\\
12&=2V_{1}-48+V_{1}\\
60&=3V_{1}\\
V_{1}&=20\ V
\end{align*}$$

### E.g. 2

![[Pasted image 20260109223456.png]]
Step 1 + 2 + 3 + 4:
Pick a reference node (ground)
Label all other nodes ($V_{1}$ and $V_{2}$)
Assume all unknown current directions are away from the node under consideration (assign polarities)
Apply KCL to each node

Node 1 ($V_{1}$):
$$\begin{align*}
0&=I+I_{R_{1}}+I_{R_{2}}\\
I_{R_{1}}&=\frac{V_{1}-E}{R_{1}}\\
I_{R_{2}}&=\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{2}}\\
0&=I+\frac{V_{1}-E}{R_{1}}+\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{2}}\\
0&=2+\frac{V_{1}-64}{8}+\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{4}\\
-2&=\frac{V_{1}}{8}-8+\frac{V_{1}}{4}-\frac{V_{2}}{4}\\
6&=\frac{3V_{1}}{8}-\frac{V_{2}}{4}\\
48&=3V_{1}-2V_{2}
\end{align*}$$
![[Pasted image 20260109224214.png]]
Node 2 ($V_{2}$):
Since $I$ goes into $V_{2}$ and $I_{R_{2}}$ and $I_{R_{3}}$ leave $V_{2}$ KCL gives us $I=I_{R_{3}}+I_{R_{2}}$ since input current = output current with KCL 
$$\begin{align*}
I&=I_{R_{3}}+I_{R_{2}}\\
I_{R_{3}}&=\frac{V_{2}}{R_{3}}\\
I_{R_{2}}&=\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{R_{2}}\\
I&=\frac{V_{2}}{R_{3}}+\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{R_{2}}\\
2&=\frac{V_{2}}{10}+\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{4}\\
2&=\frac{V_{2}}{10}+\frac{V_{2}}{4}-\frac{V_{1}}{4}\\
80&=4V_{2}+10V_{2}-10V_{1}\\
80&=-10V_{1}+14V_{2}
\end{align*}$$
Step 5:
Solve simultaneous equations 
$$\begin{align*}
3V_{1}-2V_{2}&=48\\
-10V_{1}+14V_{2}&=80
\end{align*}$$
$$\begin{bmatrix}
3 & -2 \\
-10 & 14
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
V_{1} \\
V_{2}
\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}
48 \\
80
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$M^{-1}=\frac{1}{22}\begin{bmatrix}
14 & 2 \\
10 & 3
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{bmatrix}
V_{1} \\
V_{2}
\end{bmatrix}=\frac{1}{22}\begin{bmatrix}
14 & 2 \\
10 & 3
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
48 \\
80
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{align*}
V_{1}&=\frac{416}{11} \approx 37.82\ V\\
V_{2}&=\frac{360}{11} \approx 32.73\ V
\end{align*}$$
### E.g. 3

![[Pasted image 20260109225544.png]]
Step 1, 2, 3, 4:
Pick reference node
Label all other nodes ($V_{1}$ and $V_{2}$)
Assume all unknown current directions are away from the node under consideration
Apply KCL to each node

Node 1 ($V_{1}$):
$$\begin{align*}
I_{A}&=I_{R_{1}}+I_{R_{3}}\\
I_{R_{1}}&=\frac{V_{1}}{R_{1}}\\
I_{R_{3}}&=\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{3}}\\
4&=\frac{V_{1}}{2}+\frac{V_{1}}{12}-\frac{V_{2}}{12}\\
48&=6V_{1}+V_{1}-V_{2}\\
48&=7V_{1}-V_{2}
\end{align*}$$
![[Pasted image 20260109225925.png]]
Node 2 ($V_{2}$):
$$\begin{align*}
0&=I_{B}+I_{R_{2}}+I_{R_{3}}\\
I_{R_{2}}&=\frac{V_{2}}{R_{2}}\\
I_{R_{3}}&=\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{R_{3}}\\
0&=2+\frac{V_{2}}{6}+\frac{V_{2}}{12}-\frac{V_{1}}{12}\\
-24&=2V_{2}+V_{2}-V_{1}\\
24&=V_{1}-3V_{2}
\end{align*}$$
Step 5:
Solve simultaneous equations

$$\begin{align*}
7V_{1}-V_{2}&=48\\
V_{1}-3V_{2}&=24
\end{align*}$$
$$\begin{bmatrix}
7 & -1 \\
1 & -3
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
V_{1} \\
V_{2}
\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}
48 \\
24
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$M^{-1}=-\frac{1}{20}\begin{bmatrix}
-3 & 1 \\
-1 & 7
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{bmatrix}
V_{1} \\
V_{2}
\end{bmatrix}=-\frac{1}{20}\begin{bmatrix}
-3 & 1 \\
-1 & 7
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
48 \\
24
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{align*}
V_{1}&=6\ V\\
V_{2}&=-6\ V
\end{align*}$$

If $V_{1}=6\ V$ and $V_{2}=-6\ V$, voltage across $R_{3}=12\ V$  
If $I_{R_{3}}$ flows from $V_{1}$ to $V_{2}$, $\Large I_{R_{3}}=\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{3}}=\frac{6-(-6)}{12}=1\ A$ 

### E.g. 4

![[Pasted image 20260109231041.png]]
Step 1, 2, 3, 4:
Pick reference node
Label all other nodes 
Assume unknown current directions away from node under consideration 
Apply KCL to each node

Node 1 ($V_{1})$:
$$\begin{align*}
0&=I_{A}+I_{R_{1}}+I_{R_{3}}+I_{R_{4}}\\
I_{R_{1}}&=\frac{V_{1}}{R_{1}}\\
I_{R_{3}}&=\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{3}}\\
I_{R_{4}}&=\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{4}}\\
0&=I_{A}+\frac{V_{1}}{R_{1}}+\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{3}}+\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{R_{4}}\\
0&=6+\frac{V_{1}}{5}+\frac{V_{1}}{3}-\frac{V_{2}}{3}+\frac{V_{1}}{2}-\frac{V_{2}}{2}\\
-180&=6V_{1}+10V_{1}-10V_{2}+15V_{1}-15V_{2}\\
-180&=31V_{1}-25V_{2}
\end{align*}$$
![[Pasted image 20260109231509.png]]
Node 2 ($V_{2})$:
$$\begin{align*}
I_{B}&=I_{R_{2}}+I_{R_{3}}+I_{R_{4}}+I_{R_{5}}\\
I_{R_{2}}&=\frac{V_{2}}{R_{2}}\\
I_{R_{3}}&=\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{R_{3}}\\
I_{R_{4}}&=\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{R_{4}}\\
I_{R_{5}}&=\frac{V_{2}}{R_{5}}\\
I_{B}&=\frac{V_{2}}{R_{2}}+\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{R_{3}}+\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{R_{4}}+\frac{V_{2}}{R_{5}}\\
7&=\frac{V_{2}}{4}+\frac{V_{2}}{3}-\frac{V_{1}}{3}+\frac{V_{2}}{2}-\frac{V_{1}}{2}+\frac{V_{2}}{8}\\
168&=6V_{2}+8V_{2}-8V_{1}+12V_{2}-12V_{1}+3V_{2}\\
168&=-20V_{1}+29V_{2}
\end{align*}$$
Step 5:
Solve simultaneous equations

$$\begin{align*}
31V_{1}-25V_{2}&=-180\\
-20V_{1}+29V_{2}&=168
\end{align*}$$
$$\begin{bmatrix}
31 & -25 \\
-20 & 29
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
V_{1} \\
V_{2}
\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}
-180 \\
168
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$M^{-1}=\frac{1}{399}\begin{bmatrix}
29 & 25 \\
20 & 31
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{bmatrix}
V_{1} \\
V_{2}
\end{bmatrix}=\frac{1}{399}\begin{bmatrix}
29 & 25 \\
20 & 31
\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}
-180 \\
168
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{align*}
V_{1}&=-\frac{340}{133} \approx -2.56\ V\\
V_{2}&=\frac{536}{133} \approx 4.03\ V
\end{align*}$$
