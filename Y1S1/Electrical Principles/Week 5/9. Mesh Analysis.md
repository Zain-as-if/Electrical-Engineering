Useful for finding unknown currents in circuits with voltage sources

Uses KVL to find the unknown currents

Results in a set of simultaneous equations that can be solved using matrices 

Number of mesh currents needed to analyse the network will be equal to the number of 'windows' of the circuit 
![[Pasted image 20260109201728.png]]

## Technique

1. Draw circulating currents in a clockwise direction in each window or loop 
2. Put + and - signs at each end of resistors (to indicate relative potential or voltage) according to the direction of the loop currents
	-  Be sure to put the +/- in the loop under consideration as common elements have two sets which may be different
3. Apply KVL to the loops in a clockwise direction
4. Solve simultaneous equations

# Solving Simultaneous Equations Using Determinants

Can be used to find variables in two or more simultaneous equations

Consider:
$$\begin{align*}
a_{1}x+b_{1}y=c_{1}\\
a_{2}x+b_{2}y=c_{2}
\end{align*}$$
The a's are the coefficients of the x's and b's the coefficients of the y's

Following is the matrix of those coefficients:
$$\begin{bmatrix}
a_{1} & b_{1}\\
a_{2} & b_{2}
\end{bmatrix}$$

The determinant (D) of a matrix is: 
$$D=a_{1}b_{2}-b_{1}a_{2}=\det \begin{vmatrix}
a_{1} & b_{1}\\
a_{2} & b_{2}
\end{vmatrix}$$
![[Pasted image 20260109203720.png]]

$$\begin{align*}
a_{1}\times b_{2}&=a_{1}b_{2}\\
b_{1}\times a_{2}&=a_{2}b_{1}\\
D&=a_{1}b_{2}-b_{1}a_{2}
\end{align*}$$
To find x in the following equations:
$$\begin{align*}
a_{1}x+b_{1}y=c_{1}\\
a_{2}x+b_{2}y=c_{2}
\end{align*}$$

Replace a coefficients of x with the c coefficients 
$$\begin{vmatrix}
a_{1} & b_{1} \\
a_{2} &  b_{2}
\end{vmatrix} \rightarrow \begin{vmatrix}
c_{1} & b_{1} \\
c_{2} & b_{2}
\end{vmatrix}$$
Set x equal to:
$$x=\frac{\begin{vmatrix}
c_{1} & b_{1} \\
c_{2} & b_{2}
\end{vmatrix}}{\begin{vmatrix}
a_{1} & b_{1} \\
a_{2} & b_{2}
\end{vmatrix}}$$
To find y replace b coefficients of y with the c coefficients 
$$\begin{vmatrix}
a_{1} & b_{1} \\
a_{2} & b_{2}
\end{vmatrix}\rightarrow \begin{vmatrix}
a_{1} & c_{1} \\
a_{2} & c_{2}
\end{vmatrix}$$
Set y equal to: 
$$y=\frac{\begin{vmatrix}
a_{1} & c_{1} \\
a_{2} & c_{2}
\end{vmatrix}}{\begin{vmatrix}
a_{1} & b_{1} \\
a_{2} & b_{2}
\end{vmatrix}}$$
Solve the determinants 
$$x=\frac{c_{1}b_{2}-c_{2}b_{1}}{a_{1}b_{2}-a_{2}b_{1}}$$
$$y=\frac{a_{1}c_{2}-a_{2}c_{1}}{a_{1}b_{2}-a_{2}b_{1}}$$
Solve previous equations with matrices
$$\begin{align*}
6I_{1}+4I_{2}&=-2\\
4I_{1}-5I_{2}&=6
\end{align*}$$
Solve for $I_{1}$ and $I_{2}$
$$I_{1}=\frac{\begin{vmatrix}
-2 & 4 \\
6 & -5
\end{vmatrix}}{\begin{vmatrix}
-6 & 4 \\
4 & -5
\end{vmatrix}}=\frac{(-2\times -5)-(6\times 4)}{(-6\times -5)-(4\times 4)}=-\frac{14}{14}=-1\ A$$
$$I_{2}=\frac{\begin{vmatrix}
-6 & -2 \\
4 & 6
\end{vmatrix}}{\begin{vmatrix}
-6 & 4 \\
4 & -5
\end{vmatrix}}=\frac{(-6\times -6)-(4\times -2)}{(-6\times -5)-(4\times 4)}=-\frac{28}{14}=-2\ A$$
# Better Matrix Simultaneous Equation Solver

## Technique 2x2 (2 Unknowns X,Y)

1. Find determinant 
2. Apply Formula:
$$\det(A)=ad-bc$$
$$A^{-1}=\frac{1}{\det(A)}\begin{pmatrix}
d & -b \\
-c & a
\end{pmatrix}$$
## Technique 3x3+ (3+ Unknowns X,Y,Z,...)
1. Find Cofactors, to find cofactors, for each element in matrix cross row + column, find determinant of remaining elements left, that is new value in cofactor matrix column E.g.
![[Cofactors.excalidraw]]
2. Find the adjoint of the matrix of cofactors, to do this treat the leading diagonal (C1,1 -> C3,3) as a mirror line and swap the values according to said mirror line E.g.
![[Adjoint.excalidraw]]
3. Find determinant by looking at top lines and similarly with cofactors for each top line element do:  $\text{Top Element}\times \begin{vmatrix}a & b \\c & d\end{vmatrix}$, where the determinant of the matrix of a, b, c, d is multiplied by the top element and a, b, c, d are the elements not crossed out of the columns/rows E.g.
![[Determinant.excalidraw]]
4. Use formula 
$$A^{-1}=\frac{1}{\det(A)}adj(A)$$
