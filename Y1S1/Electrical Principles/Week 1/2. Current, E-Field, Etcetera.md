# Current

Free electrons are the charge carriers in a conducting material. With enough thermal energy the valence electron becomes delocalised from the atom. W/ no external forces applied, the net flow of charge in any one direction is zero. 

If external +ve charge applied to one end of piece of conducting material, then electrons attracted to +ve charge. 

Current is the flow rate of charge ($Cs^-1$) 

Direction of electron flow 
-ve -> +ve 

Direction of current flow 
+ve -> -ve
![[Pasted image 20251124131458.png]]

However in semi-conductors the flow of charge can be either direction due to electron flow or a flow of +ve charge

Ampere - Constant current which when flowing in two infinitely long, straight thin parallel conductors spaced one metre apart in a vacuum produces a specific force of $2\cdot10^{-7}$ N/m between the conductors 

# Charge

Electrical charge is defined as the time integral of current 
$$q = \int I(t)\ dt\ \text{[A s or C]}$$ If current is constant
$$q=It$$
Unit of charge (Coulomb, C) is the charge passing a point when one ampere flows for one second
$$q_e=-1.602\cdot 10^{-19}$$
-1C is the charge of $6.242\cdot 10^{18}$
Hence if 1C flows through a cross section line of a conductor in one second, then there is one ampere (A) flowing in the conductor
