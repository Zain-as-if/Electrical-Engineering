# Coulomb's Law

The force of attraction or repulsion between two charged bodies is $\propto$ to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them: $F\propto \frac{q_{1}q_{2}}{r^2} [N]$

Constant of proportionality is usually expressed as: $$\frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_{0}}$$
$\epsilon_{0}$ is referred to as the permittivity of free space i.e. vacuum (or fundamental electric constant)

Coulomb's law is then expressed as: $$F=\frac{1}{\epsilon_{0}}\frac{q_{1}q_{2}}{4\pi r^2}=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}\frac{q_{1}q_{2}}{r^2}[N]$$
**Where Permittivity of free space is:** $\epsilon_{0}\approx 8.854\times 10^{-12}\ [C^2m^{-2}N^{-1}]\ or\ [F / m]\ or\ [m^{-3}kg^{-1}s^4A^2]$ 

<h4>Voltage</h4>
Can be considered the pressure that forces the charged electrons to flow in an electrical circuit. This flow of electrons is the electrical current that flows

The electrical potential/voltage is a measure of the electrical pressure available to force the current around a circuit
